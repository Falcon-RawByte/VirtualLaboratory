Event Detection - jumping ball Open Manual

There are models that require accurate detection of events in the system, that can in some ways alter its behaviour. A simple example of such simple is a falling elastic ball that collides with the surface. The ball itself has a starting velocity Vx and is accelarating towards the ground at 9.8m/s2. At the moment of collision the ball loses some of its energy and changes the direction. The precision which the event is detected with can has a significant influence on the result trajectory and contribute to deviation from analytical solution.
